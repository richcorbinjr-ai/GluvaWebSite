How It Will Work

Claims-Driven. Data-Proven.
CFO-Ready.

01

Identify High-Risk Employees from Claims

Gluva will analyze your TPA claims extract and PBM drug utilization data to identify employees with pre-diabetic indicators, metabolic pharmacy spend, and GLP-1 exposure — before they cost your plan more. Risk identification is driven entirely by actual claims data, not voluntary screening or health fairs.

No voluntary screening. No health fairs. Pure claims intelligence.
02

Deploy CGM-Guided Intervention

High-risk employees will receive a continuous glucose monitor and two personalized dietitian sessions anchored to their actual glucose data — not generic nutrition advice. Interventions will concentrate on the employees with the highest room for measurable improvement, making every dollar of program spend count.

Abbott Lingo CGM. Two-session dietitian protocol. 90-day program.
03

Link Outcomes Back to Claims

After one plan year, Gluva will pull an updated claims extract and compare the intervention cohort against a waitlist control group. The difference in cost trajectory becomes your documented ROI — in cost-per-member terms your CFO can present at any board meeting or stop-loss renewal.

Peer-reviewed methodology. Publishable results. Board-ready reporting.
ROI documentation requires completed TPA data integration and a minimum multi-year program commitment. Results will vary based on employer plan design, cohort risk profile, and intervention adherence.

The Evidence Base

Built on Peer-Reviewed Research

Gluva's financial model is anchored to published clinical and economic studies — not vendor marketing claims. Every savings assumption traces to a named study and a documented methodology.

Diabetes Care · ADA 2025

NDPP Real-World Cost-Effectiveness

Kuo et al. — N=5,948 employees with prediabetes in employer-sponsored health plans. $4,552 in direct medical savings per enrollee over 2 years. 88% probability program was cost-saving. 2.8 percentage-point absolute diabetes risk reduction.

Primary savings anchor
JMCP · January 2025

CGM and Healthcare Utilization Outcomes

Aetna retrospective analysis — N=7,336 patients with diabetes. CGM initiation associated with −31% all-cause hospitalizations, −67% diabetes-related admissions, and −40% diabetes-related ED visits. All findings p<0.0001.

Utilization reduction basis
Diabetes Care · ADA 2024

Economic Costs of Diabetes in the US

Parker et al. — Average annual cost per diagnosed diabetes patient: $22,540 in direct medical and pharmacy costs. People with diabetes have medical expenditures 2.6× higher than those without. GLP-1 therapy adds $18,000–$30,000+ per member per year at commercial plan rates.

GLP-1 avoidance basis
Active Research Initiative

Community College Pilot Study — In Development

Gluva is developing a prospective cohort pilot study in a community college population, ages 18–22, with elevated glucose or BMI — a population that is pre-diabetic, largely unscreened, and understudied. We are pursuing an academic partnership with a major research university for IRB co-investigator designation and peer-reviewed publication. This study will be the first CGM-guided intervention documented specifically in a community college student population.
