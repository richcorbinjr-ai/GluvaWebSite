How It Will Work

Three steps from claims data
to documented impact

01

Identify from Claims

Gluva will analyze your TPA claims extract and PBM drug utilization data to identify employees with pre-diabetic indicators, metabolic pharmacy spend, and GLP-1 exposure — before they cost your plan more.

No voluntary screening. Pure claims intelligence.
02

Deploy CGM Intervention

High-risk employees will receive a continuous glucose monitor and personalized dietitian sessions anchored to their actual glucose data — not generic nutrition advice. Resources concentrate on employees with the highest room for measurable improvement.

Continuous glucose monitoring. Personalized protocol.
03

Link Outcomes to Claims

After one plan year, Gluva will pull an updated claims extract and compare participant glucose improvements against plan cost trends — looking for measurable reductions in metabolic claims spend that may support more favorable stop-loss underwriting and lower long-term health plan cost projections.

Claims-linked reporting. Stop-loss ready.
ROI documentation requires completed TPA data integration and a minimum multi-year program commitment. Results will vary based on employer plan design, cohort risk profile, and intervention adherence.

The Evidence Base

Built on peer-reviewed research

Gluva's financial methodology is anchored to published clinical and economic studies. Every savings assumption traces to a named peer-reviewed study and a documented methodology.

Diabetes Care · ADA 2025
NDPP Real-World Cost-Effectiveness
Kuo et al. — N=5,948 employees with prediabetes in employer-sponsored health plans. $4,552 in direct medical savings per enrollee over 2 years. 88% probability program was cost-saving. 2.8 percentage-point absolute diabetes risk reduction.
Primary savings anchor
JMCP · January 2025
CGM and Healthcare Utilization Outcomes
Aetna retrospective analysis — N=7,336 patients with diabetes. CGM initiation associated with −31% all-cause hospitalizations, −67% diabetes-related admissions, and −40% diabetes-related ED visits (all p<0.0001).
Utilization reduction basis
Diabetes Care · ADA 2024
Economic Costs of Diabetes in the US
Parker et al. — Average annual cost per diagnosed diabetes patient: $22,540 in direct medical and pharmacy costs. People with diabetes have medical expenditures 2.6× higher than those without. GLP-1 therapy adds $18,000–$30,000+ per member per year at commercial plan rates.
GLP-1 avoidance basis
